THE CHALLENGE

Why Commercial Silicon Cannot Serve
Sovereign Infrastructure

Export-Control Risk

Foreign governments can restrict or revoke access to commercial silicon at any time — mid-programme, mid-deployment, mid-crisis.

No Architecture Visibility

Closed firmware prevents the security audits mandated for critical infrastructure. You cannot certify what you cannot inspect.

Lifecycle Mismatch

Consumer chips are discontinued in 3-5 years. National infrastructure is planned and budgeted in decades.

No Data Sovereignty

Cloud-dependent inference violates national data residency law. Data must be processed on-device, on-shore.

No Sovereign Security Stack

No hardware root of trust, no key provisioning, no secure boot in foreign commercial SoCs at this price point.

Supply-Chain Fragility

Single-supplier dependency creates unacceptable risk. When the vendor discontinues, the programme fails.
