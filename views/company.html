Why We Built Veredian

We built chips at the frontier of compute for [years to confirm] decades - AI SoCs, automotive safety silicon, leading-edge tapeouts at Intel, Qualcomm, STMicroelectronics, Infineon, and [companies to confirm]. We know what it takes to deliver silicon that works when failure is not an option.

We also saw the same problem from the inside: the sovereign infrastructure that governments depend on - surveillance networks, port terminals, oil platforms, border systems - runs on chips designed for commercial volume by foreign companies, on foreign supply chains, with foreign interests. That is not a technology problem. It is a strategic vulnerability.

Veredian exists to remove that vulnerability. We are building a portfolio of sovereign semiconductor platforms — beginning with EDGE-VISION™ 1000, which is designed from first principles for sovereign programmes. The architecture, the security stack, the 15-year lifecycle commitment, and the IP ownership structure are purpose-built for governments that cannot afford to depend on someone else's silicon.

We are not building a product. We are building the sovereign silicon infrastructure that national programmes have needed for a decade.
