## **1. The Positioning Change — Why and How** 

A website centred on one chip says "startup with a single bet." A website centred on a sovereign silicon portfolio says "national capability partner." Sovereign buyers procure long-term silicon partners, not single SKUs — and investors value platforms over products. The change is structural, not cosmetic: 

|Company identity|"Sovereign edge AI vision SoC company"→"Sovereign semiconductor company — Sovereign Silicon,<br>Designed for Nations."|
|---|---|
|Hero headline|"Purpose-Built Silicon for Sovereign Infrastructure"→"Sovereign Silicon. Designed for Nations." The<br>mission leads; the chip follows.|
|Product architecture|One product page→a four-platform portfolio with EDGE-VISION™ 1000 as the highlighted flagship.|
|The platform story|New "Sovereign IP Foundation" narrative: NPU, ISP, security stack, and CPU chassis are shared IP<br>blocks — one audit, one certification pathway, many products.|
|Stat bar|Chip specs (8 TOPS · <5W)→company-level stats: 4 Silicon Platforms · 10 Mission Verticals · 15+ Yr<br>Lifecycle · 80+ Combined Yrs.|
|Navigation|"Product"→"Products". Footer tagline→"Sovereign Silicon. Designed for Nations."|
|Investor framing|"World's first sovereign edge AI vision SoC"→"Sovereign silicon platform — one IP foundation, a<br>family of products." New thesis card: Platform Economics.|
